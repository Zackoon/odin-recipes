Indonesian Fried Rice (Nasi Goreng)

Description

Nasi Goreng is the an Indonesian fried rice, using sweet soy sauce and shrimp paste (preferably Indonesian shrimp paste, terasi). The umami of the shrimp paste will blow you away!

Ingredients

For Spice Paste:

  • 2 small shallots (2 oz; 55g), roughly chopped
  • 3 medium cloves garlic
  • 1 large fresh green chili, like Fresno or Holland, stemmed and seeded, or 1 tsp sambal oelek, such as Huy Fong
  • 1/2 tsp shrimp paste (optional)

For Nasi Goreng:

  • 4 cups cold cooked jasmine rice (21 ounces; 600g) or other medium- to long-grain rice. Preferably a day old, but if not, lay it out on a sheet pan/plate and refrigerate beforehand for best results.
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) neutral oil, such as canola or sunflower oil
  • 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce (can be substituted to taste with dark soy sauce + sugar/oyster sauce)
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • Kosher salt
  • Ground white pepper

For serving:

  • 2 fried eggs, sunny-side up or over easy
  • Sliced cucumbers (optional)
  • Sliced cucumbers (optional)
  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Fried, crispy Shallots(optional)

Steps

  1. For Spice Paste: Add half the shallots to a mortar and grind with the pestle until a coarse pur√©e forms. Add remaining shallots, followed by garlic, chile, and shrimp paste (if using), grinding with the pestle until each ingredient is mostly incorporated before adding the next. The final paste should resemble thick oatmeal in texture. Alternatively, combine all spice paste ingredients in a small food processor and process until they form a paste.
  2. For Nasi Goreng: If using day-old rice, transfer rice to a bowl and break rice up with your hands into individual grains.
  3. Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until shimmering. Add spice paste and cook, stirring constantly and scraping the bottom of the wok or pan to prevent the paste from burning, until a pungent smell permeates your kitchen and the paste turns a few shades darker, 2 to 3 minutes. Reduce heat to medium at any time if the paste appears to be browning too quickly.
  4. Add rice to the wok and stir to coat with the spice paste. Add kecap manis and soy sauce. Stir and cook until rice is evenly colored and hot throughout. Season with salt and white pepper.
  5. Divide rice between two plates and top each plate of rice with a fried egg. Garnish with cucumber and tomato slices and shower with fried shallots, if you like. Serve immediately with kecap manis alongside for drizzling.
